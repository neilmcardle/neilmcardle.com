DESIGN SYSTEMS

Creating a Global Design System

The problem our product team faced was an inconsistency across regional apps for a global car rental brand. Part of the solution was to implement a tailored design system to unify the brand across regions, EMEA, NAM and APAC. This system would ensure a cohesive user experience for millions of users, enhancing brand recognition and operational efficiency.

Bringing Figma In-House

One problem we faced was transitioning from Figma designs, provided by an external vendor, to our in-house app team.

This bespoke design system met our exact needs, which in turn improved our regional team's agility, and deepened our product understanding. But, they still needed to be created in the development environment.

After the Figma files were handed off to me for control, maintenance and evolution of the design system across all regions (NAM, EMEA and APAC), we proceeded to develop the designs in code.

Switching from Flutter to React Native

Initially, our design system project began in collaboration with an external development agency specialising in React Native. Their expertise was crucial for transitioning the existing app functionalities from our previous Flutter development environment to a React Native framework.

The transition was strategic, aiming to unify our global development teams around a single, efficient structure for app development. With the new React Native foundation in place, we rolled out the design system across multiple regions.

This rollout was meticulously planned to ensure that while we adapted to local needs, we maintained the integrity of our brand. The design system, accessible through Figma, became the central reference for all user interface queries, ensuring that every team, regardless of their location, was aligned with our unified vision.

Throughout this process, our focus was on fostering collaboration, enhancing development efficiency, and most importantly, delivering a consistent user experience globally.

Navigating the Challenges

Initially, NAM and EMEA faced challenges with divergent UI designs due to regional differences and legacy systems.

NAM's UI was largely undocumented and scattered across development environments, while EMEA used Adobe XD, necessitating a shift to Figma for standardisation. This transition involved moving from Flutter to React, requiring significant skillset adaptation across teams.

To unify these UI systems, NAM teams transitioned into the use of our Figma design system for UI documentation, while EMEA teams underwent training to adapt to both Figma and React.

We aligned on user flows within Figma, ensuring the new design system could adapt to local needs while maintaining brand consistency.

We innovated by leveraging in app testing to bridge the gap between old and new systems, demonstrating the feasibility of the new system in a UAT environment with stakeholder engagement, running the UAT build on their own phone and receiving their feedback straight to Jira from the UAT app build.

Our continuous-updates approach involved stakeholders in the decision-making processes early on, reducing resistance to change by allowing quick improvements in the app UAT environment which stakeholders could see and experience.  
 
Each region's unique challenges—from undocumented UI in NAM to the technological shift in EMEA—were addressed through a blend of strategic foresight, innovative technology use, and thorough engagement with all involved parties, leading to a globally coherent design system.

Anticipating the Impact

The anticipated impacts of launching the new app include streamlining development processes and significantly reducing maintenance costs through a unified codebase and design system.

To deeply assess user satisfaction and engagement, we're implementing an in-app feedback form. This feature isn't just about collecting data; it's about fostering a dialogue with our users, allowing them to directly influence the product's evolution through qualitative feedback and detailed comments on any issues or suggestions.

We're optimistic that the new consistent user experience will not only elevate user happiness but also translate into a substantial uplift in Net Promoter Scores (NPS). An improved NPS reflects not just satisfaction but also loyalty and advocacy, indicating how likely users are to recommend our app to others.

NPS, while only knowable if it has improved post-launch, will be a key indicator of our success in creating a more user-centric, cohesive application.

What Could Be Next?

The design system is not static but will evolves alongside our business needs.
‍
The AI-driven personalisation will certainly include tailoring user experiences by learning from interactions, customising layouts, content, and themes without explicit user input, but through AI insights.

Enhanced Accessibility could focus on deeper accessibility, like advanced voice control, refined colour contrasts, and possibly enhanced haptic feedback for better inclusivity.

Maybe even emerging technologies could integrate into the app, involving blockchain integration to enhance data security by providing immutable records, decentralisation of high-value data, enhanced privacy through controlled access, and automated compliance via smart contracts.

In the immediate future, our improved approach to a unified design system will make sure we remain relevant, allow for innovation, and help align our user's expectations with cutting-edge technology trends.
