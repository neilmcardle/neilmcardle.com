# CURRICULUM.md — UK National Curriculum Content Map

This document maps every statutory curriculum topic to a planned interactive tool. It is the single source of truth for what gets built and in what order.

All objectives are drawn from the DfE National Curriculum programmes of study (2014, updated to reflect current statutory guidance).

---

## How to read this document

Each entry has:
- **Topic** — the curriculum area
- **NC Objectives** — direct quotes from the statutory programme of study
- **Tool Type** — what kind of interactive experience to build
- **Tool ID** — the unique identifier used in routes, database, and components
- **Priority** — P1 (build first), P2 (second phase), P3 (later)

## Year 3

### Science

#### Plants
**NC Objectives:**
- Identify and describe the functions of different parts of flowering plants: roots, stem/trunk, leaves and flowers
- Explore the requirements of plants for life and growth (air, light, water, nutrients from soil, and room to grow) and how they vary from plant to plant
- Investigate the way in which water is transported within plants
- Explore the part that flowers play in the life cycle of flowering plants, including pollination, seed formation and seed dispersal

**Tool:** Interactive flowering plant diagram — tap each part to reveal its function; plant growth simulator with adjustable variables (water, light, nutrients)
**Tool ID:** `y3-science-plants`
**Priority:** P1

---

#### Rocks
**NC Objectives:**
- Compare and group together different kinds of rocks on the basis of their appearance and simple physical properties
- Describe in simple terms how fossils are formed when things that have lived are trapped within rock
- Recognise that soils are made from rocks and organic matter

**Tool:** Rock classification sorter (drag rocks into categories); animated fossil formation sequence; soil layers explorer
**Tool ID:** `y3-science-rocks`
**Priority:** P1

---

#### Light
**NC Objectives:**
- Recognise that they need light in order to see things and that dark is the absence of light
- Notice that light is reflected from surfaces
- Recognise that light from the sun can be dangerous and that there are ways to protect their eyes
- Recognise that shadows are formed when the light from a light source is blocked by a solid object
- Find patterns in the way that the size of shadows change

**Tool:** Shadow simulator — drag a light source and object on canvas, shadow updates in real time; reflection explorer with mirror angles
**Tool ID:** `y3-science-light`
**Priority:** P1

---

#### Forces and Magnets
**NC Objectives:**
- Compare how things move on different surfaces
- Notice that some forces need contact between two objects, but magnetic forces can act at a distance
- Observe how magnets attract or repel each other and attract some materials and not others
- Compare and group together a variety of everyday materials on the basis of whether they are attracted to a magnet
- Describe magnets as having two poles
- Predict whether two magnets will attract or repel each other, depending on which poles are facing

**Tool:** Magnet poles simulator (drag poles together, see attract/repel animation); material sorter (drag objects to magnetic/non-magnetic buckets); surface friction comparison
**Tool ID:** `y3-science-forces`
**Priority:** P1

---

#### Animals Including Humans
**NC Objectives:**
- Identify that animals, including humans, need the right types and amount of nutrition, and that they cannot make their own food; they get nutrition from what they eat
- Identify that humans and some other animals have skeletons and muscles for support, protection and movement

**Tool:** Interactive skeleton — tap bones to learn names and functions; food groups nutrition sorter; muscle and movement explorer
**Tool ID:** `y3-science-animals`
**Priority:** P1

---

### Maths

#### Number — Place Value
**NC Objectives:**
- Count from 0 in multiples of 4, 8, 50 and 100
- Find 10 or 100 more or less than a given number
- Recognise the place value of each digit in a 3-digit number (hundreds, tens, ones)
- Compare and order numbers up to 1,000
- Read and write numbers up to 1,000 in numerals and in words
- Solve number problems and practical problems involving these ideas

**Tool:** Place value blocks builder — drag hundreds/tens/ones blocks to build numbers; number line explorer; compare and order drag-and-drop
**Tool ID:** `y3-maths-place-value`
**Priority:** P1

---

#### Number — Addition and Subtraction
**NC Objectives:**
- Add and subtract numbers mentally, including a 3-digit number and ones, tens, hundreds
- Add and subtract numbers with up to 3 digits, using formal written methods of columnar addition and subtraction
- Estimate the answer to a calculation and use inverse operations to check answers
- Solve problems, including missing number problems, using number facts, place value, and more complex addition and subtraction

**Tool:** Step-by-step column method animator — child enters digits, app animates carrying/borrowing; mental maths challenge with scaffolding
**Tool ID:** `y3-maths-addition-subtraction`
**Priority:** P1

---

#### Number — Multiplication and Division
**NC Objectives:**
- Recall and use multiplication and division facts for the 3, 4 and 8 multiplication tables
- Write and calculate mathematical statements for multiplication and division using the multiplication tables they know
- Solve problems, including missing number problems, involving multiplication and division

**Tool:** Times tables trainer (game-style, streak-based, covers 3s, 4s, 8s); array builder for visual multiplication; division as grouping interactive
**Tool ID:** `y3-maths-multiplication`
**Priority:** P1

---

#### Measurement — Length and Perimeter
**NC Objectives:**
- Measure, compare, add and subtract lengths (m/cm/mm); mass (kg/g); volume/capacity (l/ml)
- Measure the perimeter of simple 2-D shapes

**Tool:** Interactive ruler (drag to measure illustrated objects); unit converter; perimeter tracer for 2D shapes
**Tool ID:** `y3-maths-measurement`
**Priority:** P2

---

#### Fractions
**NC Objectives:**
- Count up and down in tenths; recognise that tenths arise from dividing an object into 10 equal parts
- Recognise, find and write fractions of a discrete set of objects: unit fractions and non-unit fractions with small denominators
- Recognise and use fractions as numbers: unit fractions and non-unit fractions with small denominators
- Recognise and show, using diagrams, equivalent fractions with small denominators
- Add and subtract fractions with the same denominator within one whole

**Tool:** Fraction wall builder; pizza/shape splitter with drag interaction; equivalent fractions matcher
**Tool ID:** `y3-maths-fractions`
**Priority:** P2

---

#### Geometry — Shapes
**NC Objectives:**
- Draw 2-D shapes and make 3-D shapes using modelling materials
- Recognise 3-D shapes in different orientations and describe them
- Recognise angles as a property of shape or a description of a turn
- Identify right angles, recognise that two right angles make a half-turn etc.
- Identify horizontal and vertical lines and pairs of perpendicular and parallel lines

**Tool:** Shape builder canvas; 3D shape viewer (rotate with drag); angle measurer; line type identifier
**Tool ID:** `y3-maths-geometry`
**Priority:** P2

---

### English

#### Spelling — Prefixes and Suffixes
**NC Objectives (lower KS2 word list):**
- Apply knowledge of prefixes: un-, dis-, mis-, re-, pre-, inter-, super-, anti-, auto-
- Apply knowledge of suffixes: -ation, -ly, -ture, -sure, -tion, -ssion, -cian
- Use morphology to work out the meaning of unknown words

**Tool:** Word builder — drag prefix/suffix tiles onto root words, definition appears; word meaning guesser game
**Tool ID:** `y3-english-prefixes-suffixes`
**Priority:** P1

---

#### Grammar — Sentence Types and Punctuation
**NC Objectives:**
- Extend the range of sentences with more than one clause by using a wider range of conjunctions
- Use the present perfect form of verbs instead of the simple past
- Choose nouns or pronouns appropriately for clarity and cohesion
- Use inverted commas and other punctuation to indicate direct speech
- Use apostrophes for possession (singular then plural)

**Tool:** Sentence builder with clause drag-and-drop; punctuation placer; speech marks trainer
**Tool ID:** `y3-english-grammar`
**Priority:** P2

---

#### Comprehension and Vocabulary
**NC Objectives:**
- Apply growing knowledge of root words, prefixes and suffixes to read aloud and understand meaning
- Retrieve and record information from non-fiction
- Discuss and evaluate how authors use language, including figurative language

**Tool:** Interactive reading passages with tap-to-define words; figurative language spotter; fact retrieval practise with non-fiction texts
**Tool ID:** `y3-english-comprehension`
**Priority:** P2

## Year 1

### Science

#### Plants (Y1)
**NC Objectives:**
- Identify and name a variety of common wild and garden plants, including deciduous and evergreen trees
- Identify and describe the basic structure of a variety of common flowering plants, including trees

**Tool:** Plant identification card game; tree type sorter (deciduous/evergreen)
**Tool ID:** `y1-science-plants`
**Priority:** P3

---

#### Animals Including Humans (Y1)
**NC Objectives:**
- Identify and name a variety of common animals including fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds and mammals
- Identify and name a variety of common animals that are carnivores, herbivores and omnivores
- Describe and compare the structure of a variety of common animals

**Tool:** Animal classification sorter; diet type (carnivore/herbivore/omnivore) drag-and-drop; body parts comparison
**Tool ID:** `y1-science-animals`
**Priority:** P3

---

#### Everyday Materials (Y1)
**NC Objectives:**
- Distinguish between an object and the material from which it is made
- Identify and name a variety of everyday materials
- Describe the simple physical properties of a variety of everyday materials
- Compare and group together a variety of everyday materials on the basis of their simple physical properties

**Tool:** Object vs material sorter; properties explorer (hard/soft, rough/smooth, transparent/opaque)
**Tool ID:** `y1-science-materials`
**Priority:** P3

---

### Maths (Y1)

#### Number — Counting and Place Value (Y1)
**NC Objectives:**
- Count to and across 100, forwards and backwards, beginning with 0 or 1, or from any given number
- Count, read and write numbers to 100 in numerals; count in multiples of 2s, 5s and 10s
- Read and write numbers from 1 to 20 in numerals and words

**Tool:** Interactive number line (tap to count); number writing practice with animated guides; skip counting game
**Tool ID:** `y1-maths-counting`
**Priority:** P3

## Year 2

### Science

#### Living Things and Their Habitats (Y2)
**NC Objectives:**
- Explore and compare the differences between things that are living, dead, and things that have never been alive
- Identify that most living things live in habitats to which they are suited
- Describe how different habitats provide for the basic needs of different kinds of animals and plants
- Identify and name a variety of plants and animals in their habitats

**Tool:** Habitat builder — drag animals and plants to matching habitats; living/dead/never-alive sorter
**Tool ID:** `y2-science-habitats`
**Priority:** P3

## Year 4

### Science

#### Electricity (Y4)
**NC Objectives:**
- Identify common appliances that run on electricity
- Construct a simple series electrical circuit
- Identify whether or not a lamp will light in a simple series circuit
- Recognise that a switch opens and closes a circuit and associate this with whether or not a lamp lights
- Recognise some common conductors and insulators

**Tool:** Circuit builder — drag wires, bulbs, switches, batteries onto a canvas; conductor/insulator tester
**Tool ID:** `y4-science-electricity`
**Priority:** P2

---

#### Sound (Y4)
**NC Objectives:**
- Identify how sounds are made, associating some of them with something vibrating
- Recognise that vibrations from sounds travel through a medium to the ear
- Find patterns between the pitch of a sound and features of the object that produced it
- Find patterns between the volume of a sound and the strength of the vibrations

**Tool:** Vibration visualiser (mic input shows waveform); pitch and volume explorer with animated string
**Tool ID:** `y4-science-sound`
**Priority:** P2

---

## Year 5

### Science

#### Earth and Space (Y5)
**NC Objectives:**
- Describe the movement of the Earth and other planets relative to the sun in the solar system
- Describe the movement of the moon relative to the Earth
- Describe the sun, Earth and moon as approximately spherical bodies
- Use the idea of the Earth's rotation to explain day and night and the apparent movement of the sun across the sky

**Tool:** Solar system orrery (animated orbits, tap planets for facts); day/night simulator (rotate Earth model)
**Tool ID:** `y5-science-earth-space`
**Priority:** P2

---

#### Forces (Y5)
**NC Objectives:**
- Explain that unsupported objects fall towards Earth because of the force of gravity acting between Earth and the object
- Identify the effects of air resistance, water resistance and friction that act between moving surfaces
- Recognise that some mechanisms including levers, pulleys and gears allow a smaller force to have a greater effect

**Tool:** Gravity drop simulator; air/water resistance racer; lever and pulley interactive
**Tool ID:** `y5-science-forces`
**Priority:** P2

---

## Year 6

### Science

#### Evolution and Inheritance (Y6)
**NC Objectives:**
- Recognise that living things have changed over time and that fossils provide information about living things that inhabited the Earth millions of years ago
- Recognise that living things produce offspring of the same kind, but normally offspring vary and are not identical to their parents
- Identify how animals and plants are adapted to suit their environment in different ways

**Tool:** Adaptation explorer — drag features onto animals for different environments; variation and inheritance trait mixer
**Tool ID:** `y6-science-evolution`
**Priority:** P3
